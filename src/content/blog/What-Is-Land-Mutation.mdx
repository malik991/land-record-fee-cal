## What Is Land Mutation?

When a property is sold, gifted, or inherited in Pakistan, the new owner's name must be officially updated in land records. This update process is called **Land Mutation**, also known as **Intiqal** in Urdu.

## ✳️ Why Is Land Mutation Important?

Land mutation legally establishes **ownership rights**. It ensures that the buyer's name is reflected in government land revenue records and allows for:

- Applying for building approvals
- Selling the property further
- Taking bank loans against property
- Protecting legal ownership in case of disputes

## ✳️ When Should You Apply for Mutation?

Immediately after the **sale deed (registry)** or **transfer of ownership** is completed, you should visit the relevant land revenue office (Arazi Record center) or (Patwari / Tehsildar) and file for mutation. This includes:

- ***Sale or purchase of property***
- ***Gifting property***
- ***Inheritance after death***
- ***Court decree***

## ✳️ How to Apply for Land Mutation?

You need the following documents:
- Registered Sale Deed / Gift Deed / Inheritance Certificate / Mutation Copy
- CNIC copies of buyer and seller
- Fard-e-Malkiat (ownership record)
- Stamp papers and affidavit in case of Deed
- Application form and two witnesses (in some cases, who have land ownership in same reveue estate) with CNIC

## 🚨 What If You Skip Mutation?

If mutation is not done, the government still recognizes the previous owner as the legal owner — even if you have paid full amount and possession.

This may cause legal issues or even property fraud.
