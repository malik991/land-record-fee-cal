<h2 className='font-semibold text-sm md:text-base mb-3'> Understanding Types of Land Mutation (Intiqal) in Pakistan </h2>


Land mutation, known as **"Intiqal" (انتقال)** in Pakistan, is the official process through which ownership rights of a property are transferred from one person to another. Mutation is recorded in the revenue records maintained by the **Board of Revenue (BOR)**.  
There are several types of mutations, each with its own legal process, documentation, and fee requirements.  
The most common are **Sale, Mortgage, Court Decree, Hiba (Gift), Tamleek/Tabadla (Family Transfer), and Inheritance (Warasat)**.

<h2 className="font-semibold text-sm md:text-lg mb-3 mt-3"> Conclusion / نتیجہ </h2>


Understanding the different types of mutations in Pakistan helps landowners and buyers ensure legal compliance and avoid disputes.  
While sale, mortgage, and court decree require formal registration, family transfers, gifts, and inheritance have simpler processes but still need to be recorded in revenue records for legal recognition.  
Always verify updated government rules before proceeding with any mutation.
