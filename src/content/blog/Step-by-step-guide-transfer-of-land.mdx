## Step-by-Step Guide to Land Transfer in Punjab, Pakistan

Transferring ownership of land in Punjab involves two legal methods:

- **Mutation (Intiqal)** – used for sale-purchase, inheritance, gift, or court decree for rural or zarai land 
- **Registry (Deed Transfer)** – used for sale and purchase of property which is under TMA boundary   

Each method requires visiting local land revenue office (Arazi Record center) or Sub-Registrar offices with proper documentation.

## ✳️ Step 1: Collect Required Documents

Depending on the type of transfer, you must gather the following documents:

- Original CNICs (seller and buyer)
- Sale deed, gift deed, or inheritance certificate
- Fard-e-Malkiat (ownership record)
- Stamp papers and affidavit (if applicable)

## ✳️ Step 2: Visit the Relevant Office

There are two types of offices:

- For **Registry (Deed):** Visit the **Sub-Registrar Office**
- For **Mutation (Intiqal):** Visit the **Arazi Record center or Patwari / Tehsildar Office**

## ✳️ Step 3: Submit Documents & Apply

After visiting the office:

- Submit the documents to the clerk/official
- Get your application of mutation entered into the computrized system
- Receive a Fee Challan and Token for processing

For mutation, the service center official (SCO) or Patwari will verify the ownership and conduct a **site inspection** if required.

## ✳️ Step 4: Appear Before Officer for Registry

In case of a **registered deed**, both parties must:

- Appear in person before the Sub-Registrar along with two witnesses 
- Read and sign the deed
- Deposit the required stamp duty and other fees 
- Complete thumb impressions and photographs

## ✳️ Step 4-1: Appear Before ADLR or Revenue Officer for Mutation

In case of a **Mutation of rural Land**, both parties must:

- Appear in person before the Revenue Officer / ADLR along with two witnesses 
- Entered the application of mutation in the computer system through SCO or Patwari
- Deposit the required Fees and other charges 
- Complete thumb impressions and photographs

## ✳️ Step 5: Finalize Mutation or Registry

Once the process is complete:

- A **Mutation number** or **Registry number** is assigned
- Your name is entered in the official land record
- You may receive a computerized ownership certificate or Fard-e-Malkiat

## 📝 Extra Tip: Online Services

In Punjab, many land services are now available online via **PLRA (Punjab Land Records Authority)**. You can:

- Verify Fard and Registry status online
- Book appointments for land mutation
- Track status of application
