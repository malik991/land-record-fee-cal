<h2 className='font-semibold text-sm md:text-base mb-3'> Difference Between Registry and Intiqal in Land Records </h2>

<p>When dealing with property transactions in Pakistan, two important terms appear repeatedly: **Registry (رجسٹری)** and **Mutation / Intiqal (انتقال)**.  
Though often confused as the same, they serve very different legal and administrative purposes. Understanding their difference is crucial for secure property ownership.</p>

<h2 className="font-semibold text-sm md:text-lg mb-3 mt-3"> 📌 What is Registry (رجسٹری) ? </h2>

<p>Registry is the **legal sale deed** of a property registered by the **Sub-Registrar Office** under the law.  
It is the strongest proof of ownership in the eyes of the court. Only a **civil court** has the power to cancel or declare it void.</p>

<div className="section-spacing">
  <Urdu>
    رجسٹری جائیداد کی **قانونی فروخت کی دستاویز** ہے جو سب رجسٹرار آفس کے تحت رجسٹر کی جاتی ہے۔  
    یہ عدالت میں ملکیت کا سب سے مضبوط ثبوت ہے۔ صرف **سول کورٹ** کو یہ اختیار حاصل ہے کہ وہ رجسٹری کو کالعدم قرار دے۔
  </Urdu>
  ---
</div>

<h2 className="font-semibold text-sm md:text-lg mb-3 mt-3"> 📌 What is Intiqal / Mutation (انتقال) ? </h2>

<p>Mutation (Intiqal) is an **administrative record update** in the **Revenue Department (Patwari / Arazi Record Center)**.  
It reflects the transfer of ownership in government land records after a sale, inheritance, or gift.  
Unlike Registry, a mutation can be challenged before higher **Revenue Officers** like **AC, DC, or Commissioner**.</p>

  <Urdu>

    یہ زمین کی ملکیت کی تبدیلی کو ظاہر کرتا ہے جیسے فروخت، وراثت یا تحفہ۔  
    رجسٹری کے برعکس، انتقال کو اعلیٰ ریونیو افسران جیسے **اسسٹنٹ کمشنر، ڈپٹی کمشنر یا کمشنر** کے سامنے چیلنج کیا جا سکتا ہے۔

<h2 className="font-semibold text-sm md:text-lg mb-3 mt-3"> 📌Additional Point (اضافی نکتہ) ? </h2>

<p>According to the Land Revenue Act, mutation (intiqal) 
cannot be performed in urban areas or within the boundaries of the **TMA (Tehsil Municipal Administration)**. 
In such cases, only **registry (registered deed)** is applicable. However, registry can be performed in any area, whether it is rural or urban. In the case of rural or agricultural (zarai) land, both mutation (intiqal) and registry can be performed.</p>

<h2 className="font-semibold text-sm md:text-lg mb-3 mt-3"> Conclusion / نتیجہ </h2>

<p>Registry and Intiqal both play important roles in land transactions.  
**Registry** provides strong legal ownership, while **Mutation** ensures administrative record updation.  
For secure property ownership, both steps are necessary – Registry for legal strength and Mutation for revenue records.</p>
