<h2 className='font-semibold text-sm md:text-base mb-3'> Understanding Sunni (Hanafi) vs Shia Inheritance in Islam </h2>


Inheritance distribution in Islam, known as **"Warasat" (وراثت)**, is derived from the Qur’an and Sunnah but interpreted differently in Sunni (Hanafi) and Shia (Ja‘fari) schools of law.  
While both emphasize justice and fairness, their rules on how shares are calculated and who qualifies as an heir vary.  
Key differences appear in treatment of grandchildren, presence of distant relatives, and gender-based shares.

<h2 className="font-semibold text-sm md:text-lg mb-3 mt-3"> Conclusion / نتیجہ </h2>


The difference between Sunni (Hanafi) and Shia (Ja‘fari) inheritance lies mainly in the inclusion or exclusion of certain heirs, and the principle of residuaries.  
Understanding both perspectives helps avoid disputes and ensures that distribution is done fairly and in line with the family’s chosen school of law.
